Efficient Management Of Scraps

Scraps encompasses non-biodegradable materials such as plastics, metals, glass, paper, and textiles.

Unlike organic waste, dry waste does not decompose easily and can persist in the environment for hundreds of years, posing significant environmental and health risks. Addressing dry waste management is essential to reduce pollution, conserve resources, and promote a sustainable future

Sources of Scraps

Major Sources Of Scrap

Households:

Everyday items such as packaging materials, newspapers, and old clothing

Commercial Establishments:

Offices, restaurants, and retail stores generate significant amounts of paper, packaging, and other non-biodegradable waste.

Construction and Demolition:

Manufacturing and production processes produce a variety of dry waste, including scrap metal, plastic offcuts, and discarded machinery parts.
